say something

I hear my doorbell go in the dead of the night
I open my eyes slowly as my pupils try to accommodate my bed lamp’s light
The doorbell? Now? At 3 am?
Who could it be?
Am I safe?
I get down from my king bed, my heart scared, and my mind searching its palace for whom it might be
So I put on my robe, take a quick glance at my globe sitting atop one of my dressers that had all of my clothing made of polyester,
and I walk towards the door awaiting to unravel the unknown
Before I get there, I head back to the kitchen.
And I know a burglar would most likely not be bell ringing, but I get hold of a knife either way
And I say to myself…if whoever is out there comes at me, then I’m about to make him or her a bleeder
And if they think I wouldn’t put up a fight, then I’m about to make them a believer
The bell goes again
And this time…this time I’m in front of the door, heart prepared, knife in hand, ready for war
I open the door slowly…and as its hinges make that annoying sound, my knife falls to the ground
And I’m trapped in between the line that separates the lost and the found.
My hands rub my eyes, I blink once, twice, lies…lies…lies!!!
But it’s true….standing before me is you.
You!
My heart becomes unprepared
And like an adrenaline junkie, questions immediately fill my head
But I’m speechless.
And asides the wind making its presence known, the air is filled with silence.
You!
How dare you?!
How dare you show up here?!
You left me here and went there!!
And you didn’t even have the compassion to tell me where
It’s been 20 years!!
Twenty!!
How dare you?
I loved you!
I cared for and cherished you!
And amidst all these thoughts…I still say nothing
And I’m waiting on you to say something
Anything!!!
Do you know what I’ve been through
Since you left, I haven’t spent a night without thinking of you
I had to sell our king-sized bed and get a twin because the space was a permanent reminder of you.
And now you’re here.
Say something!
Nothing?!
20 years and nothing?
Well, I’ve got nothing to say to you either.
So I say to myself:
They say when one door closes, another one opens.
So as slowly as I opened the door, I close it.
I’m not about to give my heart time to play a part in this decision.
I’m not about to let myself hear any words that’ll on my heart, create an incision.
But...
But what if...
What if you need me?
What if this time you’ll stay with me?
And so I turn to the door once more and turn its knob and behold it’s locked.
And I awake, for real this time.
My back bleeding sweat.
My eyes, completely wet
Myself, in my king-sized bed.
Again.
It’s happened again.
And you…still not here.
Still not here but there.
And I, still not knowing where.
